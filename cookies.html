Last Updated: July 3, 2025

1. Understanding What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. Think of them as tiny memory aids for the website, allowing it to remember your preferences, login details, or what items you've added to a shopping cart. Without cookies, websites would treat you as a new visitor every time you navigate to a new page, making the browsing experience much less convenient. They play a crucial role in modern web functionality and user experience.

2. Purpose of Cookies on Rendoor Aquariums Website

At Rendoor Aquariums, we use cookies for various purposes aimed at enhancing your experience and understanding how our website performs. Primarily, they help us ensure the website functions correctly, remember your preferences (like language or region), analyze website traffic patterns, and personalize content and advertisements that are more relevant to your interests. This data allows us to continuously improve our website's design, content, and functionality, ensuring that we provide the most engaging and efficient service possible to all our visitors.

3. First-Party Cookies Explained

First-party cookies are those set directly by the website you are visiting â€“ in this case, by Rendoor Aquariums. These cookies are essential for the basic operation of our site and are often used to remember your settings, process your orders, or maintain your login session. For instance, if you add items to your shopping cart on our Rendoor Essentials Shop, a first-party cookie remembers these items as you browse other pages. They are fundamental to providing a seamless and personalized browsing experience directly from our domain.

4. Third-Party Cookies Explained

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than the one you are visiting. These are typically used for cross-site tracking, ad-serving, or analytics purposes. For example, if we use Google Analytics to understand website traffic, Google will set a third-party cookie on your device. Similarly, if we embed content from social media platforms, those platforms might set their own cookies. While not directly controlled by Rendoor Aquariums, we ensure that any third-party services we use adhere to strict privacy standards and are used responsibly.

5. Session Cookies: Temporary Functionality

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain on your device only for the duration of your browsing session. They are automatically deleted once you close your web browser. These cookies are crucial for facilitating navigation and remembering your actions within a single visit, such as keeping you logged into your account as you move between pages or remembering items in your shopping cart before checkout. They do not collect any information from your computer or mobile device.

6. Persistent Cookies: Long-Term Preferences

Persistent cookies, also known as permanent cookies, remain on your device for a longer period, even after you close your browser. They have an expiration date set by the website that issues them, or they can be manually deleted. These cookies are used to remember your preferences and settings for future visits, such as your language choice, login credentials (if you opt for "remember me"), or personalized content. This allows for a more convenient and tailored experience upon your return to our website.

7. Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential Operations

Strictly necessary cookies are indispensable for the Rendoor Aquariums website to function correctly. These cookies enable core functionalities such as security, network management, and accessibility features. Without these cookies, certain services you request, like logging into secure areas or adding items to your shopping cart, cannot be provided. They do not collect any personal information for marketing purposes and are always active, as they are fundamental to the website's operation.

8. Performance Cookies: Website Optimization

Performance cookies, also known as analytical or statistics cookies, collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often, how long users spend on each page, and if they encounter any error messages. This data is aggregated and anonymized, meaning it does not identify individual users. We use this information to analyze website traffic, understand user behavior, and ultimately improve the performance and usability of our website, ensuring a better experience for all.

9. Functionality Cookies: Enhanced Personalization

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personalized features. For instance, they can remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other customizable parts of web pages. These cookies can also be used to provide services you have requested, such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. They aim to make your browsing experience more convenient and tailored to your preferences.

10. Targeting/Advertising Cookies: Relevant Content Delivery

14. How to Manage Cookies in Your Browser (General)

Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences through their settings. You can typically find options to accept, reject, or delete cookies. 15. Managing Cookies in Google Chrome

To manage cookies in Google Chrome, click the three-dot menu icon in the top-right corner, then navigate to "Settings" > "Privacy and security" > "Site Settings" > "Cookies and site data." Here, you can choose to allow all cookies, block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or manage specific site permissions. You can also clear all cookies and site data, or delete individual cookies. These options provide granular control over how Chrome handles cookies from various websites.

16. Managing Cookies in Mozilla Firefox

In Mozilla Firefox, click the three-line menu icon in the top-right corner, then select "Options" (or "Preferences" on Mac) > "Privacy & Security." Under the "Cookies and Site Data" section, you can choose to block third-party cookies, manage data for specific websites, or clear all cookies and site data. Firefox also offers enhanced tracking protection, which can automatically block many types of tracking cookies, giving you more control over your online privacy.

17. Managing Cookies in Apple Safari

For Apple Safari users, go to "Safari" > "Preferences" > "Privacy." Here, you can select options to "Prevent cross-site tracking," "Block all cookies," or "Manage Website Data" to view and delete specific cookies. Safari's default settings often prioritize privacy, blocking many third-party cookies by default. These settings help protect your browsing activity from being tracked across different websites, enhancing your privacy online.

18. Managing Cookies in Microsoft Edge

In Microsoft Edge, click the three-dot menu icon in the top-right corner, then go to "Settings" > "Privacy, search, and services." Under "Clear browsing data," you can choose what to clear. For more specific control, go to "Cookies and site permissions" where you can manage cookies and site data, block third-party cookies, or add specific sites to allow or block. Edge provides a comprehensive set of tools for managing your online privacy.
